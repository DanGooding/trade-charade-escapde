Define Your Instrument¶

An instrument is the term we use for the 'thing' that we are trading. As such, if we are trading BMW stocks, we would call those an instrument. However, BMW options would be a different instrument.

\n

This field determines which instrument we are trading. By changing it, you will insert trades for a different instrument. You can see all available instruments by looking at the dropdown menu labeled \"Instruments\" on the visualizer.

# Get out of all positions you are currently holding, regarless of the loss involved. That means selling whatever\n# you are long, and buying-back whatever you are short. Be sure you know what you are doing when you use this logic.\nprint(e.get_positions())\nfor s, p in e.get_positions().items():\n    if p > 0:\n        e.insert_order(s, price=1, volume=p, side='ask', order_type='ioc')\n    elif p < 0:\n        e.insert_order(s, price=100000, volume=-p, side='bid', order_type='ioc')  \nprint(e.get_positions())\n
